A Compendium of Adjectives

Systematic vocabulary, organized by semantic domain

Adjectives cluster into families. This compendium maps nine of them — emotion, character, aesthetic, epistemic, social, sensory, tonal — into tables that expose internal structure: intensity scales, virtue/vice poles, Aristotelian triads, blended dyads.

No claim to exhaustiveness. Boundaries between categories are fuzzy, and many adjectives belong to more than one register. Treat this as scaffolding, not a closed taxonomy.

IIIEmotion — the dyads

Nine tables. Roughly 900 adjectives. Boundaries are fuzzy by design — warm belongs to emotion and character; sublime bridges aesthetic and tonal; gauche shows up in three registers. The overlaps are features of the vocabulary, not defects of the taxonomy.
